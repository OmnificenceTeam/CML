Conclusions

Nilotinib is a potent inhibitor of the ABL kinase structurally similar to Imatinib and 30 times more potent in vitro than imatinib1
Nilotinib is a TKI recommended for the first line management of CP CML2
Nilotinib allows for earlier achievement of deeper molecular responses3
In ENESTnd trial, Nilotinib was associated with significantly less progressions to AP/BC compared to Imatinib4
With 6 years of follow-up, results from ENESTnd continue to support use of frontline Nilotinib 300 mg BID as a standard of care for patients with newly diagnosed Ph+ CML-CP with a safety profile that remained consistent with previous reports4
